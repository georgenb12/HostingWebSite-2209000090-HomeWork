The central problem we're addressing is evident: Here's a brief explanation of how hierarchical clustering works: Initialization:** Start by treating each data point as a single cluster. This means that initially, there are as many clusters as there are data points. Measure Similarity:** Calculate the similarity or dissimilarity between each pair of clusters. The choice of similarity metric depends on the nature of the data and the problem at hand. Common metrics include Euclidean distance, Manhattan distance, or correlation coefficients. Merge Closest Clusters:** Identify the two clusters that are most similar to each other and merge them into a new cluster. The notion of "closeness" or "similarity" is defined by the chosen metric.*Update Similarity Matrix:** Recalculate the similarity between the new cluster and the remaining clusters. This involves updating the similarity matrix to reflect the new cluster structure.
